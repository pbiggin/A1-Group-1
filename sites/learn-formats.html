Formats of Cricket

Cricket, as a sport is multi-faceted in how it can be played. Depending on the level of compeition, formality, available time and standard of play, different formats can be used to suit the needs of the game.

Types of Formats

At the international Level of cricket, there are 3 main formats: Test Matches, One-Day Internationals and Twenty20 Internationals.

Test Matches

The Traditional form of cricket. Test Cricket is a 5-day format which has two innings per day, the longest period of time for all three formats. Teams in test cricket are challenged on their endurance, temperment and techniques in different conditions to succeed.

One Day internationals

Started in 1971, One Day internationals are one inning matches of 50 overs per side, which teams are challenged to use their speed, coordination and skill to succeed.

Twenty20 internationals

The newest format of cricket (Originated in 2005), Twenty20 internationals are a format of 20 overs per side copleted in 3 hours. Twenty20 internationals are the shortest and fastest form of the game, and often leads to huge hits, intense bowling and stratergy to gain points in the short time span available.

Summary

Cricket has multiple versions of which it can be played:

  • Test Matches
  • One-Day Internationals
  • Twenty20 Internationals
