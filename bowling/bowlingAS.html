The Art of Bowling

OVERVIEW

Bowling is a popular recreational activity played by people of all ages.

The term most commonly refers to ten-pin bowling, a competitive game in which players aim to knowck down a set of ten pins using a bowling ball. Its history can be traces all the way back to anceient Egypt, where participants rolled stones at different objects with the aim of knowcking them over. Nowadays, the most popular form of the sport involves a narrow lane, pins, and bowling ball of the player's preferred size.

RULES

  • The main objective of the game is to knowck down all ten pins by throwing the ball down the lane
  • At each turn, the player is given two chances (each one is called a frame) to knock down as many pins as they can
  • At the end of their turn, the pins reset and the next player may begin their attempt and scores are recorded after each turn
  • The player with the highest score is declared the winner of the round

TERMINOLOGY

  • Strike: when all ten pins are knowcked down in one frame
  • Spare: when all ten pins are knowcked down after two frames
  • Split: when the remaining pins are positioned on either side of the lane, leaving a gap in the centre

THE SET-UP

  • Ten pins are set up in a triangular pattern with a base of 6 at the end of the lane
  • The player then selects their chosen bowling ball from the dispenser
  • The game can be played with the gutter guards up or down, depending on the players' preference
